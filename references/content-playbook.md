# Content Playbook

Use this reference when drafting or revising the article.

## Topic selection

Prefer topics with at least one of these traits:

- **Public utility**: policy, housing, education, transport, healthcare, local services.
- **Conflict**: different groups have different stakes or opinions.
- **Timeliness**: current news, seasonal needs, policy changes.
- **Share value**: readers can forward it to family, coworkers, neighbors, or friends.
- **Local angle**: connect national news to a specific city/community when possible.

If no topic is provided, return 3 candidate topics with:

1. title angle
2. why it may get traffic
3. target reader
4. risk/sensitivity note

## Title formulas

Generate at least 5 options before choosing one.

Good formulas:

- Number + benefit: `杭州人注意：这5个变化，可能影响你的钱包`
- Pain point + solution: `社保又有新变化，普通人最该看懂的是这3点`
- Counterintuitive: `为什么我劝你别急着买房？`
- Hot event + local angle: `新规落地后，杭州这些人最先受影响`
- Conflict: `有人拍手叫好，有人彻夜难眠：这项政策到底改变了什么？`

Avoid titles that are vague, purely literary, or only summarize facts.

## Opening patterns

The first 100–300 characters decide whether readers continue.

Use one pattern per article:

1. **Counterintuitive hit**: start by challenging the obvious conclusion.
2. **Data bomb**: use one specific number, then explain why it matters.
3. **Scene story**: a short everyday scene that readers recognize.
4. **Quote then turn**: cite a viral phrase/opinion, then push deeper.
5. **Question chain**: 2–3 sharp questions that force self-identification.

Avoid:

- `大家好，今天我们来聊聊...`
- long background before the point
- generic transitions like `随着时代的发展`

## Structures

Choose one structure based on the topic.

### Parallel list

Best for practical guides and quick public-service posts.

```text
Hook
Point 1 + image
Point 2 + image
Point 3 + image
Hard ending + discussion prompt
```

### Progressive analysis

Best for policy, finance, city, or social topics.

```text
What happened
Why it matters
What is really changing
What ordinary readers should do
```

### Contrast

Best for myths, disputes, and behavior advice.

```text
Wrong understanding
Correct understanding
What the gap causes
What choice the reader should make
```

### Story-led

Best for emotion, life, and human-interest topics.

```text
Short story
Hidden point
Wider phenomenon
Reader takeaway
```

## Rhythm rules

- Keep paragraphs short; most should be 1–4 lines.
- After two dense paragraphs, add one short punch line.
- Use bold sparingly: no more than 5 bold emphasis points.
- Do not overuse em dashes or heavy formatting.
- Prefer concrete nouns, examples, and scenes over abstract commentary.

## Ending patterns

Use at least one:

- memorable sentence
- clear action step
- emotional resonance
- open discussion question
- surprising reversal

Weak endings to avoid:

- `以上就是今天的分享`
- `希望对你有所帮助`
- `感谢阅读，我们下期再见`
